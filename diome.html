The Belly of the Atlantic

Nov 13, 2025 Literature Fatou Diome

Fatou Diome’s The Belly of the Atlantic explores migration, identity, and the divide between Africa and Europe. Through the story of Salie, the novel examines displacement, belonging, and the complex realities of the Senegalese diaspora.

01. The Duality of Belonging

Salie embodies a divided identity, caught between her life in France and memories of her native island. Diome portrays migration not merely as movement, but as a rupture of the self, revealing a deep longing for home tempered by realism.

“To be torn between two worlds is not to live twice, but to be halved.”

02. The Myth of Europe

Europe is often idealized as a land of wealth and opportunity, yet Diome exposes the alienation, racism, and marginalization that migrants face. Characters like Madické illustrate the disillusionment born from chasing a myth.

03. Language and Power

Diome’s prose alternates between irony and tenderness. She uses language as critique and survival, blending French and Senegalese oral traditions to reclaim narrative authority and assert a diasporic voice.

“Exile is not a place, but a rhythm — a way of breathing between two shores.”

04. Conclusion

The Belly of the Atlantic stands as a testimony of diasporic consciousness. Diome bridges continents through empathy and reflection, showing that identity transcends geography and resides in the stories we tell to survive.
