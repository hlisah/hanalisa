Hi ! I’m Hana-Lisa, I’m 21, and I currently pursuing a Master’s in Media, Culture & Society at Pantheon-Assas University after earning a degree in Literature and Media at Sorbonne University. My studies gave me both rigorous writing skills and a deep understanding of communication sciences, shaping my approach to storytelling with clarity and critical insight. My journey into journalism has been both enriching and at times frustrating. Through multiple internships, including with the former chief editor of Elle Magazine and within the digital editorial chief of La Tribune, I discovered the realities of traditional media. I have been introduced to economic reporting, which now truly fascinates me. Yet, I also faced discouragement: I was advised against pursuing classical journalism because of my literary writing style and diverse skills. This highlighted a tension between the artistic expression I value and the neutral, “flat” style expected in mainstream press, a compromise I am unwilling to make, especially after three years dedicated to literature. These experiences convinced me that I want to build an independent media outlet, one that engages young audiences, makes complex topics like economy and politics accessible, and allows writing to remain vibrant and meaningful. On this portfolio, you will find my academic work and many other projects reflecting my curiosity, commitment, and personal approach to journalism.
