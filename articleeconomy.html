Young People and the Economy

They are redefining value, work, and growth — not through numbers, but through meaning. The new economy speaks their language.

The economy of tomorrow is being written today by a generation that no longer believes in stability as a sign of success. Young people redefine the notion of work, not as a career ladder, but as a path for impact, creativity, and coherence with their values.

“We don't just want a salary — we want significance.”

The shift from ownership to purpose

For decades, economic power was measured in possessions. Today, it’s about participation. The youth economy thrives on collaboration — from co-working to community investing. Platforms like Patreon, Substack or independent creative studios embody this evolution: autonomy meets audience trust.

Work as an ecosystem

Beyond traditional hierarchies, young creators and entrepreneurs favor ecosystems where learning, sharing, and creation intertwine. The boundaries between art, media, and business blur — making economy a living, cultural force.

Rethinking value

The post-digital generation questions the meaning of productivity. What if value wasn’t only measurable in profit, but in the collective benefit of creation, education, and sustainability? From social entrepreneurship to cultural initiatives, the next economy looks less like a market, more like a movement.

“The new capital is cultural.” — HANA-LISA H.

By mixing purpose and pragmatism, young people remind us that the economy is not just about numbers — it’s about narratives. They are not waiting for the system to evolve. They are quietly rewriting it.
