Playing with Money: How Young People Bet on Crypto

May 22, 2025

Faced with a financial system they barely understand, many young people are taking matters into their own hands. Crypto and micro-investment apps have become their playgrounds — spaces to experiment, fail, and occasionally win, all while navigating an economy without a clear manual.

Lost in the Economic System

Young people want to participate in the economy, but traditional tools remain inaccessible. In 2023, the OECD reported that only 39% of French adults had adequate financial literacy to make informed decisions — a number even lower for those under 30. University courses and specialized media often focus on theory or technical jargon, leaving many to figure things out on their own.

Investing Without a Map

Lou, 22, a literature student at the Sorbonne, discovered crypto on TikTok. “I started investing six weeks ago,” she says. “It was mostly because everyone was talking about it. I’m a student, so if I could make some money, great. I knew I could lose it, too.”

She downloaded a popular app, Crypto.com, and chose Bitcoin and another token almost at random. Within a month, she made 300 euros. “It went up fast and came down just as fast. I could see the peaks, but I didn’t understand why,” Lou admits. Her small profit was less about expertise and more about chance — a reflection of how volatile crypto markets can be for newcomers.

Like many in her generation, Lou invests to experiment and feel involved in the economy, even without formal guidance. This behavior reveals both a hunger for financial autonomy and the limits of access to traditional economic knowledge. Finance becomes a mix of intuition, risk, and social influence — sometimes rewarding, often unpredictable.

“We don't just want a salary — we want significance.”
